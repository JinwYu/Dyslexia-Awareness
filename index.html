Understanding Dyslexia

Welcome

The focus here is to visualise Visual Dyslexia, not Phonological/Auditory Dyslexia. Most of the visualisations are based from information provided by Abigail Marshall (2018) on dyslexia.com, which is the website for Davis Dyslexia Association International.

A dyslexic person can have several of these symptoms listed there. You will be presented with only one visualisation of a symptom at the time. It is important to have in mind that these visualisations are just that, visualisations, they do not accurately simulate how it is to have a reading disability. The purpose is to raise awareness and to provide people without a reading disability an understanding of how frustrating it can be. These visualisations hope to serve as a way for people to understand how it feels, not to show exactly how it is.

You will be presented with a sentence that you are going to read. It is optional to submit an answer of what you think the text says. You can continue without submitting an answer. You are encouraged to read the text in the visualisations aloud as you try to read. There will be 10 visualisations.

Source: https://www.dyslexia.com/question/what-dyslexics-see (Retrieved 2018-05-11)
